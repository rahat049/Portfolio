Research

Thesis work, Wells turbine study, and academic project work.

Research Statement

Research Focus

Work focuses on ocean wave energy extraction through passive structural compliance, high-fidelity CFD, and fluid–structure interaction simulation.

The research aims to reduce the need for active control systems by studying how flexible structures can absorb and store wave-induced mechanical energy.

Research Goals

  • Coupled aero-hydrodynamic optimization of wave energy converters
  • Bio-based composite buoy materials for sustainable WEC construction
  • Real sea-state data integration in passive WEC numerical models
  • Scalable WEC deployment strategies for Bay of Bengal coastal zones

Thesis Research

Numerical Analysis of Strain Energy Extraction of a Variable-Shape Buoy Wave Energy Converter

ANSYS Fluent VOF Multiphase Transient Structural FSI Coupling Hyperelastic Material Bay of Bengal KUET · 2019–2024

Methodology

  • Built a 3D numerical wave tank in ANSYS Fluent using VOF multiphase formulation
  • Used FSI coupling between hydrodynamic pressure and transient structural response
  • Modeled a hollow spherical buoy with hyperelastic material behavior
  • Used Bay of Bengal irregular wave conditions as input parameters

Key Findings

  • Confirmed hydroelastic response during wave–structure interaction
  • Showed passive storage of wave-induced mechanical energy as strain energy
  • Captured Von Mises stress and strain-energy evolution across wave cycles
  • Built a computational framework for passive wave energy converters

Research Study

Effect of Rotor Geometry and Flow Rate on Wells Turbine Performance

ANSYS Fluent Turbomachinery OWC Systems KUET · 2023–2024

This study examined how rotor geometry and airflow rate influence Wells turbine efficiency in oscillating water column wave energy systems.

Setup

  • Tested blade count, chord length, and sweep angle variations
  • Simulated different airflow rates to observe stall behavior
  • Used SST k-ω turbulence model in ANSYS Fluent
  • Measured torque coefficient, input coefficient, and efficiency

Findings

  • Rotor geometry affected peak efficiency and stall margin
  • Higher flow rates pushed the turbine toward stall conditions
  • Geometry selection was critical for variable wave-airflow conditions
